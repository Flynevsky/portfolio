Third setup,
From july 2020 to march 2021.

Telescope
Newton Skywatcher 150/1200
My old dobson Skywatcher 150/1200, a good telescope with a good resolution for what I am doing. It's doing the job very well and I don't think buy a new telescope worth it, because it's too expensive to buy a better one and I haven't used its full potential yet. It have a resolving capacity of 0.77", a limit value (mag) of 12.7 and a light gathering capacity of 459. The tube weighs 5.8kg alone, ~6.2kg fully equipped. It cost ~300€. I have few eyepieces, two good ones from my dobson Kepler 300/1500 and two other ones from the Skywatcher 150/1200.


Mount
Equatorial Meade LXD 55
A quite precise very old mount that I bought used for ~300€. Quite light, only 10kg.


Imagers
Nikon D5200 / SVBONY SV 205
The Nikon D5200 was a great camera, I took photos of deep sky and the Moon, and large photos of the sky with it. It has a definition of 6000 x 4000 and the pixel size is 3.9µm. The SVBONY SV 205 was quite good for planetary, the quality was not very great but it cost only 90€. It have a definition of 3840 x 2160 and the pixel size is 1.4µm.




At this time, I didn't very know how to use equatorial goto mount, so I tried many times but almost all my photos were stretched and bad.




I took this photo of Saturne with the SVBONY SV 205 and it has been my better and sharpest photo for a while.




The Nikon D5200 has a very great quality and I took aa very detailed photo of the Moon, the original photo was 4000 x 4000 but I lost it.
