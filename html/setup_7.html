Seventh setup,
From December 2022 to March 2024.

Telescope
Newton Skywatcher 150/1200
Still this good old dobson Skywatcher 150/1200, a good telescope with a good resolution for what I am doing. It's doing the job very well and I don't think buy a new telescope worth it, because it's too expensive to buy a better one and I haven't used its full potential yet. It have a resolving capacity of 0.77", a limit value (mag) of 12.7 and a light gathering capacity of 459. The tube weighs 5.8kg alone, ~6.2kg fully equipped. It cost ~300€. I have few eyepieces, two good ones from my dobson Kepler 300/1500 and two other ones from the Skywatcher 150/1200.


Mount
Equatorial Skywatcher NEQ6 Pro
The same mount, a very precise equatorial mount that I exchanged for my Kepler 300/1500. This equatorial mount track stars by compensating the Earth rotation so I can take photos with long exposure, and so the imager captures more light. The payload capacity is 18 kg and the total weight (with counterweight (2x5kg), without telescope) is 33.5kg. The resolution is 0.144 arc sec, the positioning accuracy is up to 1 arc minute and the mount compensates the mechanicals errors. It featured an ST-4 Auto Guider Interface for auto guidance camera, job done by my ASI120MC-S Color caméra.


Imager
Nikon D5200
A very good DSLR camera, it really changed the quality of my photos. It has a resolution of 6000x4000.


Autoguiding
ASI 120 MC-S Color
Using my Nikon D5200 as the imager allowed me to use autoguiding, the ASI 120 MC-S Color greatly made the job.
